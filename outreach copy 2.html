Dragon Tree

The Dragon Tree, also known as Dracaena draco, is a subtropical plant from the northwest region of Africa. It is a very versitile plant and thrives in many different environments, but for best results is should be placed in indirect sunlight. The Dragon Tree doesn't need too much watering, only about once every couple of weeks, but it does need warm temperatures between 70 and 80 degrees Farenheight.

Bunny Ears Cactus

Bunny Ears cactus, also known as Angel Wings, are super easy to take care of. They come from Mexico where they have adapted to dry climate. They only need to be watered once every couple of weeks. DO NOT touch these plants with your bare hands. They look soft and inviting so I (in my infinte wisdom) picked it up bare handed and those tiny little spines sting and are impossible to get out of your hands.

Another cactus that my sister dumped on me. At least I could tell this one is a barrel cactus due to its unique shape. Barrel Cacti are native to southwestern North America. Elephant's Foot

The Elephant's Foot (not to be comfused with a pile of nuclear waste), also known as the Ponitail Palm, is one of my favorite plants. It's kinda annoying because it needs so much space because of it's crazy leaves, but it's easy to take care of and grows very quickly which is rewarding. It likes being a little root bound, so keeping it in a pot that's a little too small is where it thrives.
